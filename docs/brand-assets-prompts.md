# Brand Assets — Logo & Banner Prompt Library

A curated set of prompts to generate Facebook page assets for **NaturePulse** and **NatureFrame**, optimised for AI image generators (Gemini, Grok, DALL-E, Midjourney).

---

## 1. Why this document exists

The two pages cover **all wildlife** — not one species. Any logo featuring a specific animal (e.g. a lion) creates a brand mismatch the moment we post about owls, dolphins, or insects. Every concept here is **animal-generic** by design — silhouettes, abstract marks, multi-species compositions, or pure nature/landscape motifs.

The logos serve **two purposes**:
1. **Facebook profile picture** — displayed as a circle, ~180×180px on profiles
2. **Video watermark** — burned into every reel at **110px wide** in the bottom-right corner

That second use case is the strict one. A logo that looks great at 500×500px can become an unreadable smudge at 110px. Every prompt below is designed to survive that downscale.

---

## 2. Asset specifications

| Asset | Dimensions | Format | Used for |
|---|---|---|---|
| Profile picture (logo) | 500×500px (square) | PNG | FB profile + video watermark |
| Facebook cover banner | 1640×624px (820×312 @1x) | PNG/JPG | FB page header only |
| Watermark variant | 300×300px | PNG with subtle dark border | Burned into video (auto-resized to 110px) |

**Mobile-safe zone for banner:** Center 820×312 area shows on mobile; sides may crop. Keep page name + key imagery centered.

---

## 3. Brand identity strategy

| | **NaturePulse** | **NatureFrame** |
|---|---|---|
| Content style | Factual + listicle (energetic, educational) | Cinematic + silent (atmospheric, artistic) |
| Personality | Bold, vibrant, dynamic | Elegant, moody, contemplative |
| Color palette | Warm earth tones — amber, gold, forest green, terracotta | Cool cinematic — deep teal, charcoal, muted gold, slate blue |
| Visual energy | Active, motion, sun, life | Stillness, mist, depth, mood |
| Typography feel | Bold sans-serif, clean | Thin serif or elegant sans, generous spacing |

These differences should be readable at a glance — a viewer scrolling Facebook should immediately know which page they're looking at.

## 8. Platform-specific master tips

### Grok 2 Image (powered by Aurora/Flux)
- **Best for:** photorealistic, cinematic, atmospheric scenes
- **Default style:** photographic — explicitly say "vector" or "illustration" if you want non-photo
- **Magic words:** `cinematic`, `shot on`, `golden hour`, `depth of field`, `color grade`, `professional photography`
- **Use for:** All NatureFrame banners, NaturePulse banners, NatureFrame logos with photographic backgrounds

### Google Gemini (Imagen 4)
- **Best for:** photorealistic with strong composition control
- **Magic words:** `professional photography`, `award-winning`, `8K resolution`, `National Geographic`, `shot with [camera]`
- **Tip:** Specify aspect ratio explicitly (`1:1 square`, `16:9 widescreen`)
- **Use for:** Both banner types, photographic logos

### DALL-E 3 (ChatGPT)
- **Best for:** vector logos, illustrations, geometric design, anything with text or shapes
- **Magic words:** `vector logo`, `flat design`, `minimalist`, `behance portfolio`, `brand identity`
- **Tip:** DALL-E often adds text even when you say "no text" — explicitly add `no letters, no words, no typography`
- **Use for:** All logo concepts above (especially geometric ones — Concepts 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 on both pages)
- **Limitation:** Struggles with multi-subject photorealistic scenes — skip for the wildlife banners

### Midjourney
- **Best for:** artistic flair, stylized output, premium look
- **Parameters to add:**
  - Logos: ` --ar 1:1 --v 6 --style raw --stylize 100`
  - Banners: ` --ar 21:9 --v 6 --style raw --stylize 150`
- **Tip:** Use `--no text` to prevent text artifacts

## 9. Watermark considerations (critical)

Whatever logo you settle on, the **video watermark** (used in `assets/logos/NaturePulse_Logo.png` and `NatureFrame_Logo.png`) needs special handling because it's rendered at **110px wide** in the bottom-right of every reel.

**Hard requirements:**
1. **Recognisable at 110px** — open the image, manually resize a copy to 110px, and check it's still readable. If detail vanishes, the concept is too busy.
2. **Works on any background** — videos cycle between bright sky, dark forest, water, snow. The logo must read on all of them. Solutions:
   - Add a subtle dark **outer ring or frame** around the logo (works like a UI badge)
   - Or generate a version with a **semi-transparent dark circle background**
3. **No fine text** — anything text smaller than ~30% of the logo's height will be illegible at 110px

**Recommended workflow:**
1. Generate the logo at 500×500 using one of the prompts above
2. Open in any editor and check it at 110px — if any element disappears, regenerate
3. If needed, add a subtle dark circular border for video contrast (I can script this with ImageMagick later)
4. Save the final 500×500 PNG to `assets/logos/<PageName>_Logo.png`

## 10. Recommended generation workflow

For each page, you only need **2 final assets** to launch:

| Asset | Recommended tool | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Logo (profile pic + watermark) | **DALL-E 3** (via ChatGPT) or **Midjourney** | Best at clean geometric logos |
| Facebook cover banner | **Grok** or **Gemini** | Best at photorealistic landscapes |

**Suggested process:**
1. Pick **2 logo concepts** you like (one for each page) from sections 4 & 5
2. Generate 4-6 variations of each in DALL-E or Midjourney; pick the best
3. Pick **1 banner concept** for each page from sections 6 & 7
4. Generate 2-3 variations in Grok or Gemini; pick the best
5. Drop final files into the repo and update the Facebook pages

**Iteration tip:** If a logo concept isn't working, try the same concept in a different tool before changing concepts. DALL-E and Midjourney often interpret identical prompts very differently.
